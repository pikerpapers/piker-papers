In this discussion on *The Deprogram*, Hasan Piker describes a strategic approach to political messaging in which more moderate reformist rhetoric can function as an entry point for audiences who may later move toward more radical political ideas. In the quoted exchange, Piker says he publicly advocates social-democratic reforms, even while acknowledging that such systems benefit from what he describes as American or Western imperialism, because he views that messaging as tactically useful in the short term. He also refers to advocating what he calls a "lighter form of propaganda" alongside broader agitation.

The conversation becomes more explicit when Hakim praises what he calls a "radicalization funnel." In his description, audiences are first introduced to less threatening or more "lukewarm" socialist ideas and may later continue toward more radical ideological positions. Hakim presents Piker's role in that process positively, arguing that the key distinction is not merely introducing audiences to moderate positions, but also not preventing them from progressing further.

Hakim also references the idea of "keeping your power levels low," a phrase commonly used online to describe downplaying the more controversial aspects of an ideology in public-facing messaging. Within the discussion, the phrase is presented as part of a communication strategy: introduce ideas in a toned-down form so that new audiences are not immediately alienated, while leaving open the possibility that those audiences later adopt more radical positions.

Taken together, the exchange describes a deliberate messaging strategy in which moderate political rhetoric serves as a gateway, drawing audiences in with more broadly acceptable ideas before exposing them to increasingly radical arguments over time.

**On the concept of a radicalization pipeline**

> "I just wanted to like put emphasis on the exact point of the so-called radicalization funnel where for example Hassan and JT catch them while they're young as they say ... and then they start looking into this ... and then move forwards through the funnel get more and more informed get more and more radicalized and so on ..."

**On allowing audiences to move toward revolutionary ideas**

> "He does not stop them from progressing through the funnel and getting additional and additional information and so on ... he doesn't agitate against for example the the Revolutionary perspective and so on and so on ..."
