This file documents a clip from Hasan Piker's stream in which he appears to refer to another streamer using the term "Asmongoy." The currently linked source is an Instagram reel preserving that clip, and the entry treats it as a record of the language used in context rather than as an isolated paraphrase.

Critics describe language like this as coded antisemitic rhetoric because it leans on a familiar conspiracy trope: the idea that Jews operate as a hidden force directing non-Jews or public figures to advance their interests. In that sense, terms built around "goy" can function as shorthand for narratives long associated with white supremacist and neo-Nazi discourse, but which have also appeared in some far-left extremist online circles when that same conspiratorial framing is repackaged in ironic or meme-style language.
